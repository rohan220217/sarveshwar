Why Festivals Matter?

The creation of our rich social, cultural, and spiritual traditions, and the celebration of festivals are the result of the collective wisdom of millions of human beings who have participated in thousands of years of Sanatan journey. They have been constantly improving and reviving. All celebrations of the Sanatana tradition are deeply spiritual in nature. They collectively prepare us to reach the level of our highest possibilities. People should do this with full devotion and dedication, so they have also been made religious. But basically, they have no relation with religion. It is a subtle and effective science.

In order to open new dimensions of possibilities, our mind (मन), intellect, body, soul, and mind (चित) are all required to be in an awakened state. To make this science accessible to farmers, laborers, and tribals, it has been simplified. It is so simple that even an illiterate person can fully involve themselves in it like a yogi. It is the success of Sanatan that deep spiritual and scientific techniques have been made part of everyday life here.

Sarveshwar Choudhary

Why Sanatan...?

This photo is the simplest explanation of the basis of thousand years of continuous journey of Sanatan Hindu culture. Sanatan culture always chose the path of knowing rather than accepting or believing.

In this culture, it was never said that 'इति' means the end, but instead proclaimed 'नेति-नेति' means no end - no end. All this happened due to our great Guru and Shishya tradition. Guru, one who make you thirsty for knowledge, who taps the strings of the mind like a veena, who shapes life like a potter, who hurts like a blacksmith, who loves like a mother, whose presence makes the heart become as deep as the ocean, who frees from fear, who makes the pebble into Shiva.

Sarveshwar Choudhary
